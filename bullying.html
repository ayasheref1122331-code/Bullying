Types of Cyberbullying

Harassment

Sending offensive or threatening messages repeatedly

Defamation

Spreading false or embarrassing information

Hacking & Impersonation

Hacking accounts and posting inappropriate content

Exclusion

Deliberately excluding someone from groups

Catfishing

Creating fake accounts to deceive others

Cyberstalking

Monitoring and harassing someone continuously

How to deal with it?

  • Don't respond to offensive messages
  • Save evidence (screenshots)
  • Report the bullying account
  • Tell someone you trust

Effects of Cyberbullying

Psychological Effects

  • Anxiety and depression
  • Low self-esteem
  • Sleep and eating disorders
  • Suicidal thoughts in severe cases
  • Feelings of isolation and loneliness

Social Effects

  • Withdrawal from social activities
  • Difficulty forming friendships
  • Decline in academic or work performance
  • Damage to personal reputation
  • Loss of trust in others

How to Deal with Cyberbullying

If You're a Victim

  1. Don't respond to bullies - that's what they want
  2. Save evidence (screenshots, messages)
  3. Report the bullying account to the platform
  4. Block bullies to stop the harassment
  5. Tell someone you trust (parents, teacher, friend)
  6. Focus on positive activities you enjoy
  7. Remember the problem is with the bully, not you

If You're a Witness

  1. Don't share or repost offensive content
  2. Support the victim and let them know they're not alone
  3. Encourage the victim to report the incident
  4. Report the offensive content if possible
  5. Confront the bully politely if you feel safe
  6. Spread positive messages to counter negativity
  7. Be a true friend to those being bullied
